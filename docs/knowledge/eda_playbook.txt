Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) playbook for Yelp rating prediction

1. Start with base rates.
Look at the user's overall historical star tendencies before focusing on the target item.
Check whether the user is generally generous, harsh, or highly variable.

2. Compare user preferences to item characteristics.
Match review language, categories, price tier, service style, and useful attributes.
The best prediction signals usually come from overlap between the user's prior positive reviews and the target business profile.

3. Check recency and consistency.
Recent reviews often reveal stronger preferences than very old ones.
If the user has inconsistent ratings across similar businesses, prediction confidence should be lower.

4. Watch for sparse data.
If the user or the business has limited evidence in the local subset, say so explicitly.
Low data coverage should reduce confidence and produce a shorter, more conservative explanation.

5. Look for contradictory signals.
A user may like a cuisine but dislike noisy venues, weak service, or poor value.
Strong categories alone are not enough if attributes suggest a mismatch.

6. Distinguish evidence from inference.
Grounding comes from exact user, item, and review records.
Inference is the crew's explanation of why those records imply a likely rating.
Never present inference as if it were directly observed.

7. Summarize EDA findings clearly.
A good EDA summary explains:
- the user's baseline rating tendency
- the strongest matching features
- the biggest mismatch or risk
- the confidence level and why

8. Keep prediction outputs operational.
The final answer should be strict JSON, with a realistic short review and a compact explanation of the predictive signals.

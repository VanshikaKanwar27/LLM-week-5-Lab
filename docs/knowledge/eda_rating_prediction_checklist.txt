Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Checklist For Rating Prediction

1. Start with base rates.
- Check the user's average stars and review count.
- Check the item's stars and review count.
- Treat both as anchors before inferring anything from style.

2. Compare user preference to item profile.
- Look for category fit, pricing fit, service expectations, and common review themes.
- Strong matches usually support ratings near the user's base rate or the item's base rate.
- Strong mismatches can justify moving the prediction down.

3. Use semantic review retrieval to find analogs.
- Search for reviews that look similar in tone, complaint type, praise type, cuisine, or service context.
- Prefer semantically relevant review evidence over generic intuition.

4. Watch for sparse data.
- Few reviews, low metadata coverage, or weak semantic matches should reduce confidence.
- When evidence is thin, stay closer to stable priors rather than making extreme predictions.

5. Draft realistic output.
- The review should sound plausible for the retrieved user behavior and the retrieved business context.
- Avoid invented sensory details or claims that were never supported by the retrieved evidence.
